The data set being used in this web app is from the CORGIS Dataset Project, linked here. This data contains statistics regarding SAT Scores across America between 2005-2015, this includes statistics regarding scores in both the Math and Verbal sections, scores associated with GPA, familial income, and gender, as well as the amount of years studied and the average GPA of different school subjects.

As mentioned previously, this data includes the average score of both sections of the SAT Exam, giving more detailed statistics as to what group of individuals scored on a specific portion of the test. When viewing the scores as seperated between the two sections, the score will be in a range of 200-800 instead of the normal 400-1600 score range you would usually see with the SAT Exam. This is because for each half section, you can only score up to 800, as that is half of the total SAT Exam score.
